# Go-to-market & growth

Distribution kills more good products than competition does. A product no one can find or be sold doesn't matter how good it is. Pick a motion that fits what you sell, and find one channel that works before chasing more.

## The motion follows the price and the buyer

Your average contract value (ACV) and who decides largely determine how you must sell:

| ACV / buyer | Motion |
|---|---|
| Low ACV, individual user | **Self-serve / product-led (PLG):** the product sells itself; users sign up, activate, and pay without a human. Needs low friction and fast time-to-value. |
| Mid ACV, team buyer | **Inside sales / sales-assist:** a rep helps close; often PLG bottom-up that converts to team plans. |
| High ACV, business buyer | **Field sales / enterprise:** demos, pilots, procurement, long cycles; a salesperson is essential because the deal can't close itself. |
| Two-sided | **Marketplace:** solve the chicken-and-egg by concentrating on one side / one geography first. |
| Community-driven | **Community-led:** value and acquisition flow through a community you cultivate. |

A common failure is mismatching: trying to sell a $50/mo product with a $5,000-cost enterprise motion, or expecting a $100k product to sell itself with no sales team.

## Channels: find one, then add

There are a finite set of acquisition channels (content/SEO, paid ads, virality/referral, sales/outbound, partnerships, community, PR, app stores, etc.). Early on:
- **Don't spread thin.** Most startups have *one* channel that drives the majority of growth. Find it, then exploit it before diversifying.
- **Test cheaply and measure.** Run small experiments per channel; judge by cost per acquired *retained, paying* customer, not by clicks.
- **Match the channel to where customers already are** and how they want to buy.

## The growth engine (Lean Startup)

Sustainable growth comes from one of a few engines — know which is yours:
- **Viral** — users bring users (viral coefficient > 1). Growth is built into product use.
- **Sticky** — high retention; you grow because you keep almost everyone and add more.
- **Paid** — you spend to acquire and earn more than you spend over the customer's life.

Optimize the engine you actually have rather than borrowing tactics from a different one.

## Don't scale a leaky bucket

Before pouring fuel on growth:
- **Retention first.** If users churn, acquisition just spins. Fix the core experience and activation.
- **Activation matters most early.** The path from sign-up to first real value ("aha moment") is the highest-leverage funnel step; many products lose people before they ever experience the value.
- **Unit economics must point the right way** — see startup-metrics. Profitable growth needs LTV > CAC with a tolerable payback period.

## A note on timing
Going to market loudly before the product retains can waste your one shot at a first impression and your best channels. Earn PMF (or strong signal) first; then scale the motion that's working.
