# Ideation techniques

A toolbox for the diverge phase. Pick two or three per session; don't grind one. The goal is range and surprise, not a tidy list.

## Generators (make more, weirder)

- **Forced connection.** Pair the subject with a random noun, object, or image and list what they have in common or how one could behave like the other. Friction is the point.
- **SCAMPER.** Run the subject through: Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify/Magnify, Put to another use, Eliminate, Reverse. Each verb is a fresh angle.
- **What-if escalation.** "What if it were 10×? 1/100th? alive? illegal? free? the last one on earth?" Push to absurdity, then retreat to the most interesting viable point.
- **Domain transplant.** Borrow the deep structure of an unrelated field — a heist, a wedding, a courtroom, a recipe, a migration, a video game tutorial — and map it onto your subject.
- **Opposite day.** State the conventional approach, then do the inverse. Quiet luxury vs. maximalism; an ad that tells you not to buy.
- **Constraint injection.** Add an arbitrary, severe rule: one color, one sentence, no faces, only sounds, must work upside down. Constraints route around your defaults.
- **Persona lens.** Re-approach as a specific maker: a child, a brutalist architect, a stand-up comic, a 17th-century cartographer, a conspiracy theorist. Each carries a worldview.
- **First principles.** Strip the subject to its irreducible function, then rebuild from scratch ignoring how it's "always done."

## Combiners (collide and recombine)

- **Mash-up matrix.** List 5 attributes down, 5 contexts across; explore the surprising cells.
- **Bisociation.** Hold two unrelated frames at once (the literal and the metaphorical) and find the image that satisfies both.
- **Exquisite corpse.** Build in passes where each pass can't fully see the last — deliberate discontinuity sparks accidents worth keeping.

## Pressure (find the charge)

- **The "so what" stress test.** For each idea, ask "so what?" until it either reveals a real hook or collapses. Survivors have substance.
- **Subtraction.** Remove the most expected element. What's left is often more original and more honest.
- **Make it specific.** Replace every general noun with a particular one: not "a bird," a one-legged pigeon; not "music," a detuned music box. Specificity manufactures vividness and truth.

## Hygiene

- Set a quota (e.g., 15 ideas) and hit it before judging anything. The first third are clichés, the middle third are interesting, the last third is where the gold hides — you only reach it by pushing past the comfortable stop.
- Write ideas as fast as they come, ugly and unpunctuated. Editing kills momentum.
- Steal structures, never finished work. Influence transformed is originality; influence copied is theft.
